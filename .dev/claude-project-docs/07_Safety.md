## COMT Intermediate - Avoid

**Basis**: COMT V158M A/G = catecholamines clear 40% slower

| Avoid | Reason | Alternative |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **High-dose Methylcobalamin** | Methyl donor loading | Low-dose or hydroxocobalamin |
| **SAMe (high dose)** | Major methyl donor | Avoid completely |
| **TMG (Trimethylglycine)** | Methyl donor | Avoid completely |
| **Betaine** | Methyl donor | Avoid completely |
| **Tyrosine** | Dopamine precursor | Avoid completely |
| **L-DOPA** | Direct dopamine precursor | Avoid completely |
| **Mucuna Pruriens** | Contains L-DOPA | Avoid completely |

**Note**: Stimulant effects feel "stronger and longer" due to slower clearance.

## GABAergic Paradox Risk - ASD-Specific

**Basis**: ~50% of autistic individuals show paradoxical excitation to GABAergic compounds.

### Risk Levels

| Risk | Compounds | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **HIGHEST** | Benzodiazepines, Z-drugs (Ambien), alcohol, phenibut, direct GABA | **AVOID** |
| **HIGH** | Valerian, hops, passionflower, kava | **AVOID** |
| **MODERATE** | Apigenin, magnolia bark, L-theanine >400mg | Test cautiously |
| **LOWER** | Taurine, glycine, magnesium glycinate | Generally safe |

### Safe Sleep Alternatives

| Option | Mechanism | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Glycine 3g | Inhibitory (different receptor) | No paradox risk |
| Magnesium glycinate | Non-receptor mechanism | No paradox risk |
| Melatonin 0.3mg | Circadian | Timing critical (8:30 PM) |
| Taurine 1-2g | Indirect GABA modulation | Safer profile |

## Caffeine Restrictions

**Basis**: CYP1A2 lower activity (slow metabolizer)

| Guideline | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| **<200mg daily** | Slow clearance extends half-life |
| **Before 10 AM only** | Afternoon affects sleep |
| **~100-150mg optimal** | 1 cup coffee morning only |

---

## B-Vitamin Excess - Avoid

**Basis**: Historical B6/Biotin elevation (May 2025) from AG1 supplementation
- B6: 102.9 μg/L (2x upper limit)
- Biotin: 7.78 ng/mL (elevated)
- **Root cause**: AG1 mega-dose B-vitamins

| Avoid | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| **AG1** | Mega-dose B-vitamins - caused toxicity |
| **Mega-dose B-complex** | B6/Biotin overload |
| **B6 >25mg** | Toxicity risk |
| **Biotin >1000mcg** | Previous elevation |
| **Energy drinks with B-vitamins** | Hidden B content |

**Note**: Verify normalization via blood work before any B supplementation.

## Iron Supplementation - Caution (Low Priority)

**Basis**: HFE H63D C/G (heterozygous carrier) - **30% clinical impact likelihood**
- Genotype unverified (K2 status)
- Iron markers currently normal per clinical assessment

| Guidance | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| **Avoid iron supplements** unless deficiency confirmed | General caution |
| **Choose iron-free multivitamins** | Standard practice |
| **Monitor ferritin periodically** | Routine screening |

**Note**: Low priority concern - iron markers normal. Include in routine blood work monitoring.

## Key Lessons Learned

### 1. Training Volume > Cannabis for Lean Mass
- Lean mass loss (4.53 lbs) correlated with training crash (73%), NOT cessation
- **Action**: Maintain 5-6 hrs/week resistance throughout cessation

### 2. 76 Days Insufficient for FAAH Variant
- First cessation attempt was below genetic threshold
- **Action**: 120-day minimum, 150-day optimal

### 3. AG1 Caused B-Vitamin Toxicity
- AG1 caused B6 (102.9) and Biotin (7.78) elevation
- **Action**: Avoid mega-B products, verify levels before supplementing

### 4. KPU Hypothesis Ruled Out
- Test: 3.65 mcg/dL (normal range)
- Continue symptomatic supplements without KPU-specific dosing

### 5. M3 DEXA During Resumption
- M3 DEXA was 46 days AFTER resumption, not during cessation
- **Action**: Correctly contextualize all data relative to cannabis status

### 6. Elite Metrics Despite Cannabis Use
- 54 BPM RHR, 43ms HRV achieved during use periods
- Training/protocol factors may be more influential than substance use
