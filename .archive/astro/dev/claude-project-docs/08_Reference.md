## ASD/ADHD Protocol Considerations

### Key Physiological Differences

**ASD-Specific:**
- GABAergic paradox (~50%): Sedatives may cause excitation
- Altered gut permeability: Zonulin 3x higher
- HRV ceiling: Lower absolute values are normal (35-45ms target)
- Track HRV trendline, not absolute value

**ADHD-Specific:**
- Delayed circadian phase (~75%): Natural bedtime 1-2 hrs later
- Flattened cortisol rhythm: Different stress response
- DAO variants (78%): Histamine sensitivity

### GABAergic Paradox Assessment

You may have this if:
- Alcohol makes you wired/anxious instead of relaxed
- Sleep medications cause agitation
- Benzodiazepines increase anxiety
- Valerian or kava causes restlessness

### ADHD Circadian Protocol

| Parameter | Standard Advice | ADHD-Optimized |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Dose** | 3-10mg | **0.3-0.5mg** |
| **Timing** | 30 min before bed | **3+ hrs before desired sleep** |
| **Purpose** | Sedative | **Chronobiotic (phase-shifting)** |

**Example for 10:00 PM sleep:**
- Take 0.3mg melatonin at 7:00 PM
- Dim lights after taking
- Maintain 7 days/week

### Circadian Anchors

| Anchor | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Fixed wake time** | Same time 7 days/week |
| **Morning light** | Bright light within 30 min |
| **Caffeine timing** | Morning only, before 10 AM |
| **Meal timing** | Regular schedule |

### Safe Sleep Stack (ASD-Optimized)

*See 04_Supplements.md Tier 2 Sleep Stack for complete protocol.*

Key compounds: Glycine 3g, Mag glycinate 300mg, Taurine 500-1000mg, Melatonin 0.3mg @ 8:30 PM

## Family Genetics Guidelines

*Simplified shareable guide for relatives*

### Always Avoid

| Substance | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| **Alcohol** | High liver disease risk + paradox effects |
| **Tylenol** | Liver toxicity—use ibuprofen sparingly |
| **High caffeine (>200mg)** | We metabolize slowly |
| **Afternoon caffeine** | Cut off by 10 AM |

### Be Cautious With

| Substance | Issue | Alternative |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| Sleep aids (Ambien, benzos) | May cause agitation | Magnesium, glycine |
| Valerian, kava, hops | Paradox risk | Chamomile tea usually fine |
| Energy drinks | Too much caffeine + B-vitamins | Coffee AM only |
| High-dose B-vitamins | Toxicity potential | Food sources |

### Supplements That Help

| Supplement | Dose | Why |
|------------|:----:|-----|
| Vitamin D3 | 2000-5000 IU | Most deficient |
| Omega-3 | 2g | Heart/brain (family risk) |
| Magnesium | 300mg evening | Sleep, stress |
| Methylfolate | 400mcg | Better for our genetics |

### Family Sleep Tips

- **Fixed wake time** every day
- **Morning sunlight** within 30 min
- **No caffeine after 10 AM**
- **Melatonin 0.3-0.5mg** only, 3 hours before bed
- **Wind down 2-3 hours** (we process stress slower)

### Liver Protection (Family Priority)

We have elevated genetic risk for fatty liver disease:
- **No alcohol**
- **No Tylenol**
- **Limit sugar/fructose**
- **Consider**: Vitamin E 400 IU, choline

### Watch For These Signs

| If You Notice... | It May Mean... |
|------------------|----------------|
| Caffeine keeps you up all night | Slow metabolizer |
| Alcohol makes you anxious/wired | Paradox response |
| Sleep aids don't work/backfire | GABAergic paradox |
| B-vitamins make you jittery | Sensitivity |
